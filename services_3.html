Production · Client incident

Desk phone down: fault isolated, not guessed

Situation
A staff member's desk phone would not come online. To the office it looked like "the network is broken again".
Diagnosis
Swap testing along the chain: a known-good phone worked on the same cable, wall port and switch port. The fault was a failed LAN port on the handset itself, not the network.
Result
Unit replaced and reprovisioned on the phone system with no extended downtime, and no time wasted rebuilding a network that was never broken.
Reference design · Lab-validated

20-user office network, designed from zero

Brief
A typical small office: finance and operations must be kept apart, guest Wi-Fi must never touch company data, and remote staff need a secure link back to the office.
Design
Segmented networks (VLANs) per department, controlled routing between them, DHCP and DNS, access rules deciding exactly who reaches what, and an encrypted site-to-site VPN. Built and validated in my lab to the same standard I deploy for clients.
Result
Departments isolated, guest traffic sandboxed to internet-only, and the whole design documented so any future IT provider can pick it up.
